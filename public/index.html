Neurobiology

The Bilateral Anchor

The research basis is that ADHD often involves persistent stimulus-seeking and difficulty stabilizing attention. A moving binaural sound field gives the nervous system something to track in the background, reducing the urge to generate new distractions in the foreground.

Bilateral Stimulation

Slow left-right motion alternates auditory emphasis across hemispheres, creating a steady tracking task that can feel grounding rather than distracting.

Neural Entrainment

When tempo, panning cycles, and subtle interaural offsets are stable, the soundscape can support phase-locking and a more durable focus state.

Non-Pharmacological Adjunct

This is framed as a support tool for self-regulation and task initiation, not a replacement for clinical treatment.
